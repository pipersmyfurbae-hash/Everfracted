What it is

Your wreath exists on
paper before it exists
in the world.

Every element you place on the canvas is drawn directly from your inventory, so material costs are calculated in real time as you design. There are no surprises when you reach the workbench.

Layer system

Build your design
from the ground up.

Every wreath has a structure. Blueprint Studio mirrors that structure with a five-layer design system: Base, Foliage, Focal Points, Accents, and Ribbon. Each layer is independently toggleable, reorderable, and individually costed.

Work on one layer at a time without losing sight of the whole. Toggle layers on and off to focus on placement, then reveal the full composition to check balance and proportion.

Layer visibility is non-destructive — hiding a layer never removes its elements or costs from your blueprint.

Live cost engine

Every element you add
is costed instantly.

Blueprint Studio is connected directly to your Inventory Tracker. As you add elements to your canvas, their unit costs are pulled from your live inventory and totalled in real time. You see your material cost grow with every placement decision.

When inventory prices change — because your supplier raised their rates, or you found a better source — your blueprint costs update automatically. No manual recalculation required.

Low-stock items are flagged directly in the canvas with a warning indicator, so you can substitute before you commit to a design.

Design canvas

A canvas built for
the circular form.

Blueprint Studio's canvas is designed specifically for wreath construction. The circular form is the primary unit — elements are placed radially, with snap-to-ring guides that mirror how a real wreath is built: from the base outward, layer by layer.

Adjust the canvas to any standard wreath size, from 12" to 36", and the element placement scales proportionally. Switch between blueprint view, colour story view, and element list view without leaving the canvas.

The canvas is non-destructive. Every placement decision is undoable, and blueprint versions are saved automatically so you can always return to an earlier state.

The Blueprint Studio workflow

From blank canvas
to finished blueprint.

Five steps from opening Blueprint Studio to having a client-approved design ready for the workbench.

1
Choose a template or start fresh

Select one of 18 professionally designed blueprint templates, or open a blank canvas at your preferred wreath size. Templates include seasonal designs, classic styles, and structural archetypes.

Studio & above
2
Build your layer structure

Start with the base layer and work outward. Add foliage elements from your inventory, place focal flowers, scatter accents, and finish with ribbon. Each layer is independently editable.

Studio & above
3
Review costs and stock

As you design, the cost engine totals your material costs in real time. Low-stock items are flagged inline. Adjust your design until the cost and availability are exactly right.

Craft & above
4
Export and share with your client

Export a PDF or generate a shareable link. Your client can review the blueprint, see the element list and colour story, and give approval — all before you start building.

Studio & above
5
Publish to the Marketplace

Once your blueprint is complete, publish it to the Evercrafted Marketplace. Other makers can purchase your design, download the layout, and build it themselves — you earn a royalty on every sale.
