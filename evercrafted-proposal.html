Section 01

The Problem

Home decor enthusiasts and professional wreath makers operate in a fragmented, analog world. The gap between creative intent and finished product is wide — and expensive.

6+
Vendor sites per order
~40%
Materials wasted on misbuys
0
Dominant SaaS tools for this niche
  • 🗺️
    Fragmented Sourcing
    Makers source stems, ribbons, bases, and wire from 6+ separate vendors — Afloral, Amazon, Michael's, Save-On-Crafts, Etsy suppliers, local wholesalers. There is no unified purchasing hub or compatibility engine.
  • 🧠
    Designs Live in Their Heads
    Experienced wreath makers carry layout knowledge intuitively. This knowledge cannot be systematized, shared with an assistant, handed to a student, or replicated at scale without a structured design system.
  • 💸
    No Pricing Intelligence
    Most craft sellers underprice their work. Without a tool that accounts for materials cost, labor hours, overhead, and market positioning, profit margins are estimated — usually badly.
  • 👁️
    Zero Pre-Build Visualization
    There is no way to see a finished wreath before purchasing materials. Every design is a financial bet. Mismatched color palettes and proportional errors aren't discovered until after materials are wired and irreversible.
  • 📆
    Seasonal Timing is Reactive, Not Planned
    Holiday wreaths require 6–8 weeks of lead time for design, sourcing, and construction. Most makers start too late, purchase reactively, and miss the peak sales window. No tool exists to systematize seasonal planning.
The Opportunity in Plain Terms
No SaaS product owns the wreath-making category. The tools that exist (Cricut Design Space, Canva, Pinterest) are generic. None speak the language of FFLGA classification, polar coordinate placement, or stem-count-to-diameter ratios. The domain moat is real and unoccupied.

Platform Architecture

Section 04

Key Features

Each module is a distinct app — purchasable individually, or included in a subscription tier. Every app speaks the language of wreath making.

  • 🔵
    Blueprint Generator Studio
    Mathematically structured wreath layouts generated from design inputs. Uses polar coordinate placement, golden angle stem distribution, and FFLGA (Focal / Filler / Line / Greenery / Accent) classification to output a balanced radial blueprint with exact stem positions, quantities, and optical balance score. Output is a printable canvas layout with layering order and construction sequence.
  • ✨
    AI Design Visualizer Atelier
    Pre-build visualization of the finished wreath from blueprint data. Generates a photorealistic render using AI image generation, alongside a Midjourney-ready prompt compiled from the design's color palette, stem types, style profile, and base type. Reduces materials waste and dramatically increases buyer confidence for commissioned pieces.
  • 📦
    Inventory Intelligence Engine Craft
    Users input on-hand materials — stems, ribbons, bases, wire gauges, accents. The engine maps available inventory to compatible blueprint templates, filters designs by what's achievable with current stock, and flags what needs to be sourced before building. Design sessions start with reality, not wishful thinking.
  • 💰
    Pricing Calculator Craft
    Inputs: total materials cost, labor hours, overhead percentage, target margin, and market positioning (budget / mid / luxury). Outputs: retail price, wholesale price, Etsy listing price, and per-unit profit. Adjusts dynamically as inputs change. Includes a price-check against comparable Etsy listings (via affiliate data) to validate competitiveness.
  • 🌿
    Seasonal Collection Builder Studio
    A constrained design workspace for building cohesive seasonal product lines. Users define a palette, style profile, and target holiday or season — the builder generates 3–6 complementary blueprint variants that share visual DNA. Output is a full collection package: blueprints, materials list, pricing sheet, and an Etsy listing draft.
  • 📋
    Builder Instructions Generator All Tiers
    Converts any blueprint into plain-language, layer-by-layer construction steps. Shareable with an assistant, a student, a wholesale buyer's production team, or a DIY kit customer. Output includes wire attachment methods, placement sequence, and finishing instructions specific to the base type (grapevine vs. pine, foam, wire).
Section 05

User Engagement

Engagement tools are designed to make Evercrafted part of a maker's regular creative routine — not just a tool they open once and forget.

Engagement Tool 01
Design Journal
A private portfolio log for every completed wreath. Each entry captures: photo upload, materials used (pulled from Inventory Engine), sell price, customer notes, and a quality rating. Over time the journal becomes a personal business intelligence dashboard — average margin per piece, most-purchased stems, best-performing seasonal styles.
Engagement Tool 02
Seasonal Calendar
Prompts users 6–8 weeks before each major holiday with curated design ideas, trending style lookbooks, a materials checklist, and a countdown to peak sales window. Reduces the reactive scramble that causes late sourcing, rushed builds, and missed revenue.
Engagement Tool 03
Community Lookbook
An opt-in gallery where subscribers share finished designs. Filtered by season, style, base type, or skill level. Drives retention through social validation, generates aspirational content, and surfaces organic upgrade motivation when users see what Studio or Atelier tier produces.
Engagement Tool 04
Skill Progression Tracker
A gamified mastery system built on the four tier names as progression milestones: Bloom → Craft → Studio → Atelier. Users earn progress by completing tutorials, logging designs, and hitting business milestones (first sale, first collection, first wholesale order). Milestone celebrations include shareable achievement cards and personalized upgrade prompts.
Retention Strategy
Why Churn Will Be Low
Seasonal tools create natural re-engagement cycles — users who churn in February return in September for Christmas prep. Design Journal history and saved blueprints create switching costs (your designs live here). The Inventory Engine, once populated with a maker's full stock, is painful to abandon. These three vectors together create sticky, habitual platform use — not just occasional visits.

Tutorial System

Structured as a learning path, not a content dump. Every lesson is tied to a tier, a skill level, and a real output — not just theory.

Design Principle
Tutorials are gated by tier — lower tiers see lesson previews with a lock + upgrade CTA, never a blank page. The preview shows the lesson title, learning outcome, and a blurred screenshot. This creates aspiration, not frustration.

Section 07

Materials Sourcing

Sourcing integration is the bridge between design intent and physical execution. It also represents one of the highest-margin, lowest-effort revenue streams on the platform.

Core Concept
The Blueprint-to-Cart Pipeline
Every blueprint generated contains a full materials list: stem types, quantities, base type, wire gauge, and ribbon yardage. That list should be one click away from a purchase. The platform's sourcing layer converts design output into a shoppable cart — capturing purchase intent at its peak.
  • 📋
    Auto-Generated Shopping Lists
    Blueprint output includes a formatted shopping list with estimated cost per item, total materials cost, and direct purchase links to preferred supplier partners. Users can export as PDF, share the list, or send directly to a procurement assistant.
  • 🏪
    Curated Supplier Directory
    A rated directory of vetted suppliers organized by category (stems, bases, ribbon, wire, tools) and quality tier (budget, mid, premium). Each supplier entry includes: average ship time, minimum order, quality rating from verified buyers, and seasonal availability notes. Updated quarterly.
  • 📦
    Bulk Order Calculator
    For production-volume makers building 20+ wreaths per season. Enter a design and target quantity — the calculator outputs total materials needed, optimal order quantities to hit supplier minimums, estimated unit cost at volume, and projected total spend. Integrates directly with the Pricing Calculator.
  • 🔔
    Seasonal Availability Alerts
    Push and email alerts when key seasonal materials come back in stock or reach peak availability — dried pampas grass, fresh eucalyptus, preserved magnolia leaves, velvet ribbon restocks. Users can set watch lists for specific SKUs or material categories. Reduces the reactive sourcing scramble before major holidays.
  • 🔄
    Inventory Auto-Deduct
    When a user marks a blueprint as "built," the Inventory Intelligence Engine automatically deducts used materials from their on-hand stock. The system flags when a material drops below a user-set reorder threshold and surfaces a one-click reorder to their preferred supplier.
Long-Term Opportunity — Confidence 70%
If supplier relationships scale, the platform could negotiate exclusive wholesale pricing for Atelier-tier subscribers — giving professional makers a cost advantage unavailable anywhere else. This would be a meaningful tier differentiator and a strong retention lever for the top subscription level.

The 30-Second Competitor Analysis
Why Existing Tools Can't Fill This Gap
Cricut Design Space — SVG-based cutting machine software. Doesn't model 3D floral placement, FFLGA logic, or stem counts. Wreath makers use it for bow templates only.

Canva — 2D graphic design. Beautiful for Etsy mockups, useless for construction blueprints or inventory management.

Pinterest — Pure inspiration, zero execution. No tools, no commerce, no structure.

Florist SaaS (BloomNation, Hana POS) — Designed for retail floral shops, not artisan wreath makers. Overkill on POS features, completely missing on design and blueprint tools.
